Tips & Recommendations: Making It Work

February 25, 2025 7 min read By Sarah Mitchell
Source: Unsplash

After multiple visits, I've learned the best times to go, what to bring, what to skip, and how to navigate the experience efficiently. These aren't generic travel tips—they're specific observations about Dubai Pool that could save you time, money, and frustration.

Timing: When to Visit

This is probably the most important factor in determining your experience. I've been at different times of day, different days of the week, and different times of year. Here's what I've learned:

Best Times of Day

Early morning (8-10 AM): This is when the facility is at its quietest. You'll have your pick of loungers, the pool is less crowded, and the temperature is more comfortable. The downside: it's early, and the restaurant might not be fully operational yet.

Late afternoon (4-6 PM): The crowds start thinning out, and the temperature becomes more pleasant. The light is great for photos, especially at the infinity edge. This is my preferred time slot now.

Midday (11 AM-2 PM): This is peak time. It's crowded, hot, and the best spots are taken. Unless you've rented a cabana or arrived very early, you'll be competing for space. I'd avoid this time if possible.

Best Days of the Week

Weekdays (Monday-Thursday) are significantly less crowded than weekends. Friday and Saturday are the busiest, and Sunday can be hit or miss. If you have flexibility, go on a weekday. The difference in crowd levels is noticeable.

Seasonal Considerations

Winter months (November-March) are the most pleasant in terms of temperature, but they're also the busiest tourist season. Summer months (June-September) are less crowded but much hotter. The shoulder months (April-May, October) offer a good balance.

What to Bring

Based on my experiences, here's what you actually need versus what you can skip:

Essential Items

  • Sunscreen (high SPF): The sun is intense, and shade is limited. Bring your own—the shop prices are marked up significantly.
  • Water shoes or flip-flops: The pool deck gets very hot, and the changing room floors are wet. Regular shoes aren't practical.
  • Hat and sunglasses: Even with an umbrella, you'll want these for moving around.
  • Water bottle: You can bring your own water, and staying hydrated is important.
  • Quick-dry towel (optional): They provide towels, but if you prefer your own, a quick-dry one is useful.
  • Cash or card for final payment: Remember, everything goes on your wristband, but you'll need to pay when you leave.

Nice to Have

  • Waterproof phone case or dry bag
  • Book or e-reader (the Wi-Fi can be slow)
  • Portable charger (charging stations exist but can be busy)
  • Snacks (though you can't bring them in, so eat before or budget for food)

What Not to Bring

  • Outside food or drinks (except water)—they check bags
  • Alcohol—not allowed
  • Large bags—lockers are standard size
  • Expensive jewelry—better left at home or in a secure locker

Booking and Planning

I always book in advance, even for weekdays. While walk-ins are sometimes possible, booking guarantees entry and can sometimes get you a small discount. The booking system is straightforward—you can do it online or by phone.

If you're planning to rent a cabana or daybed, book that in advance too. The good ones get reserved quickly, especially on weekends. The rental cost is separate from admission, so factor that into your budget.

Check the weather before you go. They close during sandstorms, which happen more often than you'd think, especially in summer. A quick check of the weather forecast can save you a wasted trip.

Navigating the Facility

The layout isn't immediately intuitive, so here's a quick orientation guide:

  • Entrance/Reception: Ground floor, where you check in and get your wristband
  • Changing Rooms: Ground floor, near the entrance
  • Main Pool: First level, accessible from changing rooms
  • Infinity Edge Section: First level, connected to main pool
  • Relaxation Pool: First level, separate from main pool
  • Restaurant: Second level, indoor, air-conditioned
  • Poolside Bar: First level, near the main pool

Once you're oriented, it's easy to navigate. The facility isn't huge, so you won't get lost, but knowing the layout helps you plan your day better.

Budgeting Tips

The admission fee is just the start. Here's a realistic budget breakdown based on my visits:

  • Admission: 150-200 AED per person (varies by day and season)
  • Food: 80-120 AED for a main course, 40-60 AED for appetizers
  • Drinks: 25-45 AED per drink
  • Cabana rental: 300-500 AED (optional)
  • Daybed rental: 600-800 AED (optional)

For a full day with lunch and a few drinks, budget around 300-400 AED per person, not including cabana rental. The wristband payment system makes it easy to overspend, so keep track mentally or check your balance periodically.

Making the Most of Your Visit

Here are some strategies I've developed over multiple visits:

  • Arrive early for the best spots: Even on weekdays, the prime loungers are gone by 11:30 AM.
  • Use the relaxation pool strategically: It's less crowded and perfect for unwinding after being in the main pool.
  • Time your meals: The restaurant is less busy outside peak hours (12-2 PM, 5-7 PM).
  • Take breaks: The sun is intense, so take breaks in the shade or indoors.
  • Stay hydrated: It's easy to forget when you're having fun, but dehydration is a real risk.
  • Reapply sunscreen: Even waterproof sunscreen needs reapplication, especially after swimming.

Common Mistakes to Avoid

I've made some of these mistakes myself, so learn from them:

  • Not booking in advance and finding it's fully booked
  • Arriving at peak time and struggling to find a good spot
  • Forgetting sunscreen and having to buy it at inflated prices
  • Not bringing water shoes and having to walk on hot deck
  • Overspending because the wristband system makes it easy
  • Not checking the weather and arriving during a sandstorm closure
  • Bringing outside food and having it confiscated at the entrance

Final Thoughts

Dubai Pool is a solid experience if you go in with the right expectations and preparation. It's not a hidden gem or a secret spot—it's a well-known facility that delivers a good experience when you know how to navigate it.

The key is timing, preparation, and realistic expectations. Arrive early, bring what you need, budget appropriately, and you'll have a great day. Arrive at peak time without preparation, and you might leave frustrated.
